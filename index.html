My Story

The path here wasn't linear.

I grew up in Santa Cruz, California—a beach kid who loved riding my bike around town so much I didn't get my driver's license until 18. (Why would I? My little beach town had everything I needed.)

Technology wasn't the plan. Education and leadership development were my focus. But after some life pivots, I found myself working in a hospital, pursued my Master's in Healthcare Administration, and got pulled into early EHR implementation work. Healthcare technology found me—and I've been here ever since.

These days, the creative spark that drew me to applied arts in high school has come full circle. I'm fascinated by how AI enables new kinds of problem-solving and creativity.

When I'm not working, you'll find me in California's Gold Country—taking long walks, enjoying good coffee and artful dark chocolate, tending my garden, and yes, hanging out with my chickens. After years in bigger places, the small-town exhale feels like home. I guess it always stuck with me.

Family is everything. Married 23 years, two grown sons (22 and 20), and a one-year-old grandbaby who makes it all worth it.

Activiteez

Activity and resource management for multi-location organizations

Calendar View
Building Selector
Inventory Catalog
Attendance View

The Problem

Senior living facilities manage dozens of recurring activities across multiple buildings — often with spreadsheets, paper calendars, and guesswork about supplies.

The Solution

A platform that handles scheduling, inventory tracking with automatic consumption, attendance management, and multi-building data isolation. Built for the activity director who doesn't have an IT department.

What I Learned

Multi-tenant architecture sounds abstract until you're debugging why Building A can see Building B's bingo cards. I learned to think in contexts — and to let AI help me catch the edge cases I'd miss.

GovernIQ

Enterprise work management and capacity planning

The Problem

Organizations tracking dozens of initiatives across multiple teams struggle to see who's overloaded, what's stuck, and where to focus. Spreadsheets break down. Visibility disappears.

The Solution

A configurable platform for initiative tracking, governance workflows, and real-time capacity analytics. Fully customizable fields, weights, and branding — no code changes required.

What I Learned

The hardest part of enterprise software isn't the features — it's making it flexible enough to fit how different organizations actually work. I built a system where admins can change everything without touching code.

VytalPath Academy

Training platform for new healthcare staff

The Problem

New front office staff face an overwhelming first day — medical jargon, insurance workflows, HIPAA compliance, clinic procedures. The result is anxiety, mistakes, and high turnover.

The Solution

A training and reference platform with 1,000+ healthcare terms in plain language, step-by-step workflows, HIPAA modules, and smart search. Accessible on phone or desktop.

What I Learned

Training isn't about dumping information — it's about being there at the moment someone needs an answer. I built for the person Googling "what does PRN mean" during their first week.

Let's Talk

I didn't go to coding bootcamp. I didn't get a CS degree. I learned to build by building — with AI as my toolbox, my debugger, and my teacher.

Now I help others do the same.

This isn't a course about using AI. It's a hands-on program where you'll build a working application — your idea, your problem to solve — using the same tools and methods behind Activiteez, Sophia, and GovernIQ.

Here's the secret: I don't know everything. Nobody does. But I've learned how to use AI to fill the gaps — to ask the right questions, validate my assumptions, and debug problems I couldn't solve alone. I'll teach you to do that too.

What you'll learn

  • How to scope an idea that's actually buildable
  • Setting up your environment (Cursor, Claude, Supabase, Vercel, GitHub)
  • Prompting strategies that get AI to build with you, not just for you
  • Iterating when things break (they will)
  • Shipping something real

Who this is for

Professionals who see AI changing their field and want to learn to build, not just watch from the sidelines. No coding experience required — just a problem you want to solve and the willingness to get your hands dirty.
